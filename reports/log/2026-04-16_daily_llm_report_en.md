### One-sentence summary

Observation of giant room-temperature third-order electrical transport induced by quantum geometry in altermagnetic RuO2 thin films.

### Problem

Exploring T-odd and T-even quantum geometric effects induced by symmetry breaking in altermagnets and finding nonlinear transport tools to probe magnetic order in compensated systems.

### Approach

Fabricating (101)-oriented RuO2 epitaxial films, performing harmonic transport measurements, and combining field-cooling, symmetry analysis, and DFT calculations to study the origins of third-order responses.

### Main takeaway

RuO2 thin films exhibit a giant room-temperature third-order Hall effect that is T-odd and reverses with Néel vector redistribution, serving as a sensitive probe for altermagnetic order.

### Why it matters

This work demonstrates altermagnets as a versatile platform for exploring quantum geometry and building room-temperature quantum devices, adding a new dimension to antiferromagnetic spintronics.

### Problem

### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

The study demonstrates the coexistence of global and local band flatness in twisted monolayer-bilayer graphene, leading to continuous correlated metallic states at non-integer fillings.

### Problem

Strong correlations in moiré systems are typically associated with global flatness at integer fillings; the mechanism for correlations at non-integer fillings driven by local flatness and its interplay with global flatness remains poorly understood.

### Approach

Using transport measurements of longitudinal and Hall resistance in twisted monolayer-bilayer graphene (tMBG), combined with an analytical Stoner model and continuum model calculations to analyze symmetry-breaking states at various fillings.

### Main takeaway

Local band flattening (driven by van Hove singularities) induces valley polarization without opening a global gap, resulting in continuous correlated metallic states with anomalous Hall signatures at non-integer fillings, distinct from the insulators at integer fillings.

### Why it matters

It establishes 'dual-flatness' as a new design principle for correlated quantum matter, extending moiré physics from discrete integer fillings to continuous non-integer regimes and identifying topological transport as a probe for gapless correlated states.

### Value assessment

- **Major advance**
- The study successfully distinguishes and unifies two different flat-band correlation mechanisms within a single material system and discovers robust valley-polarized states at non-integer fillings.
