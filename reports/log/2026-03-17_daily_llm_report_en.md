### One-sentence summary

Observation of two-component exciton Bose-Einstein condensates with spin-valley degrees of freedom in MoSe2/hBN/WSe2 van der Waals bilayers.

### Problem

Although exciton condensation has been theorized for decades, direct experimental evidence for equilibrium condensates with rich internal degrees of freedom in solid-state systems has remained elusive.

### Approach

Using magneto-optical spectroscopy in a dilution refrigerator to probe the layer-resolved spin-valley susceptibility of electrons and holes in MoSe2/hBN/WSe2 electron-hole bilayers.

### Main takeaway

Discovery of three exciton condensate phases, including a two-component intravalley condensate at zero field and a field-driven first-order quantum phase transition to an intervalley condensate, with transition temperatures up to 1.8 K.

### Why it matters

This work establishes van der Waals bilayers as a versatile platform for exploring strongly interacting, multicomponent exciton BECs and high-temperature superfluidity, analogous to superfluid helium or atomic spinor condensates.

### One-sentence summary

Flat bands are generated in vertically engineered quad-layer InAs/GaSb quantum wells, providing a twist-free and scalable platform for correlation physics.

### Problem

Conventional twistronics faces challenges such as twist-angle disorder, strain, and lack of scalability or reproducibility.

### Approach

Using MBE to grow quad-layer heterostructures, designing band inversion via k·p modeling, and characterizing effective mass through magnetotransport and FIRMS.

### Main takeaway

The quad-layer InAs/GaSb structure produces flatter bands near the Gamma point than bilayers, with experimentally confirmed significantly enhanced effective mass.

### Why it matters

This approach leverages mature semiconductor growth to create flat bands, offering a highly reproducible system for exploring superconductivity and strong correlations without twist-related issues.
