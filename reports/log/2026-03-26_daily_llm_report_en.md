### One-sentence summary

This paper comparatively studies exciton properties in monolayer WSe2 using real space and Landau quantization space, revealing the competition between magnetic fields and Coulomb interactions in determining exciton state composition.

### Problem

How to understand the internal composition of bound excitons in terms of free electron-hole Landau levels under a magnetic field, and to verify the consistency between real-space and Landau-quantization-space approaches.

### Approach

Using the effective mass approximation and Keldysh potential, the exciton energy spectrum, diamagnetic coefficients, and wavefunction composition are calculated in both real space (radial equation) and Landau quantization space (matrix diagonalization).

### Main takeaway

The Landau-level composition of exciton states is determined by the competition between the magnetic field, which drives the dominant component toward higher-index pairs, and Coulomb interactions, which drive it toward lower-index pairs.

### Why it matters

This study provides a microscopic perspective on non-hydrogenic exciton behavior in 2D semiconductors, explains experimental energy spectrum evolution, and paves the way for analyzing exciton states in complex systems like moiré superlattices.

### Value assessment

### Problem

### Approach

### Main takeaway
