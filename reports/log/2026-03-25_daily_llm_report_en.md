### One-sentence summary

Investigates topological pumping in a photonic Hofstadter system, revealing that edge transport persists through Landau-Zener transitions even when bulk states are localized by strong quasiperiodic modulation.

### Problem

Exploring the interplay between localization induced by strong quasiperiodic modulation and topological transport, specifically how pumping mechanisms evolve when bulk states are no longer extended.

### Approach

### Why it matters

Demonstrates that topological robustness persists despite bulk localization, providing a new route to study complex topological phases, non-adiabatic dynamics, and transport in disordered systems using compact photonic platforms.
