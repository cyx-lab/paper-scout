### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

This study reveals that KPZ scaling in exciton-polariton condensate arrays originates from the stochastic dynamics of Nambu-Goldstone modes arising from spontaneous symmetry breaking.

### Problem

While KPZ universal scaling has been observed in polariton systems, a microscopic explanation linking system parameters like interaction strength and pump power to this behavior was missing.

### Approach

The authors used an analytical model based on the Bogolyubov ansatz and numerical simulations to calculate the first-order coherence function in 1D and 2D polariton lattices and extract critical exponents.

### Main takeaway

KPZ scaling stems directly from Goldstone mode fluctuations, and this non-equilibrium dynamics is most prominent at low pump powers where fluctuations are comparable to the condensate density.

### Why it matters

It bridges microscopic physical quantities and macroscopic universality, providing a theoretical basis for controlling the coherence properties of quantum light sources by manipulating Goldstone modes.

### Value assessment

- **Mechanism clarification**
