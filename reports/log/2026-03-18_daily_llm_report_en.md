### One-sentence summary

The study reveals that the pseudogap in doped Mott insulators is a uniform altermagnetic state driven by kinetic interactions within the t/U expansion of the Hubbard model.

### Problem

Explaining the microscopic origin of the pseudogap phase in cuprates, specifically its position between antiferromagnetism and superconductivity and its lack of a clear order parameter.

### Approach

Using the t/U series expansion of the Hubbard model combined with static mean-field theory and time-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation to analyze the impact of kinetic terms (especially the three-body term) on altermagnetism, superconductivity, and charge orders.

### Main takeaway

A uniform d-wave altermagnetic state naturally occupies the pseudogap region of the phase diagram, with its metastable boundary mimicking the T* line, and it is highly unstable toward inhomogeneous orders like pi-flux and bond currents.

### Why it matters

It connects the emerging concept of altermagnetism to the forty-year-old mystery of the cuprate pseudogap, providing a physically realistic microscopic foundation for understanding intertwined orders in high-temperature superconductivity.
