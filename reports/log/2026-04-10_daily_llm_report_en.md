## Optical Hall absorption sum rule and spectral compensation in time-reversal-breaking moiré and Hofstadter systems

### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

Investigates the optical Hall absorption sum rule and spectral compensation in time-reversal-breaking moiré and Hofstadter systems.

### Problem

Lack of exact constraints on antisymmetric Hall absorption in topological states, making it difficult to distinguish between internal and field-induced topological responses.

### Approach

Derived the first-frequency-moment sum rule for Hall conductivity and performed theoretical analysis and numerical verification using moiré continuum models (with Hartree-Fock and BSE) and Hofstadter models.

### Main takeaway

The first moment of Hall absorption vanishes in zero-field moiré systems, requiring spectral compensation, whereas in magnetic systems, it is universally fixed by magnetic flux density.

### Why it matters

The sum rule provides a rigorous, contact-free framework for quantifying circular dichroism, diagnosing Landau-level mixing, and identifying the microscopic origin of topological response.

### Value assessment

- **Mechanism clarification**
- Clarifies the sum rule constraints on optical Hall response in different topological systems and reveals the nature of spectral compensation and its link to Landau-level mixing.

## Chirality of Zitterbewegung and its relation to Berry curvature in Dirac systems

- **categories:** cond-mat.mes-hall

- **summarized_at:** `2026-04-10T09:24:10`

### Related topics

quantum_geometry:berry curvature

### One-sentence summary

Establishes an exact analytical relation between Zitterbewegung dynamics and Berry curvature in 2D Dirac systems.

### Problem

Zitterbewegung originates from interband coherence while topological properties are defined by intraband geometry; a clear analytical link between them was missing.

### Approach

Defined a time-independent antisymmetric observable called the areal rate of Zitterbewegung and linked it to the quantum geometric tensor using projector decomposition.

### Main takeaway

The sense of rotation (chirality) of Zitterbewegung is directly determined by the sign of the Berry curvature and reproduces the Chern number contributions from Dirac points.

### Why it matters

It provides a new method beyond the semiclassical regime to probe band topology and quantum geometry through nonequilibrium dynamical observables.

### Value assessment

- **Mechanism clarification**
- The study successfully unifies seemingly independent dynamical phenomena and topological geometric properties, providing a solid analytical basis for understanding quantum dynamics in Dirac systems.
