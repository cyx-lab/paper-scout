### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

This study investigates epitaxial MnPt3 thin films, demonstrating that the anomalous Hall effect is dominated by the intrinsic Berry curvature mechanism and significantly tuned by strain effects.

### Problem

Although the XPt3 family is theoretically predicted to be topological semimetals with nodal lines, the anomalous Hall transport properties of MnPt3 lacked systematic experimental investigation.

### Approach

Epitaxial MnPt3 thin films (20–70 nm) were grown on MgO substrates via magnetron sputtering, followed by scaling analysis of magnetization and transport measurements to distinguish intrinsic and extrinsic contributions.

### Main takeaway

The anomalous Hall effect in MnPt3 is dominated by the intrinsic Berry curvature mechanism, and the intrinsic anomalous Hall conductivity increases with thickness, primarily attributed to the effective tuning of electronic band topology by strain.

### Why it matters

This work fills the experimental gap in the topological transport of MnPt3 and demonstrates that strain engineering is an effective tool for tuning Berry curvature and transport properties in topological magnetic materials.

### Value assessment

- **Major advance**
- It provides the first experimental verification of the intrinsic anomalous Hall effect in MnPt3 and establishes a quantitative link between thickness, strain, and topological transport.

### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

The study demonstrates that the excitonic Mott transition in monolayer WSe2 can occur without population inversion or optical gain.

### Problem

Traditional theories link the excitonic Mott transition to population inversion and optical gain, but it is unclear if this paradigm holds under ultrafast non-equilibrium excitation.

### Approach

Combined femtosecond pump-probe spectroscopy with real-time ab initio simulations based on the non-equilibrium Green’s function (NEGF) formalism.

### Main takeaway

The excitonic Mott transition is driven by the interplay of strongly non-thermal carrier populations and non-equilibrium dynamical screening, showing that population inversion is not a universal prerequisite for exciton dissociation.

### Why it matters

The findings challenge conventional wisdom in semiconductor physics and provide a new framework for understanding many-body dynamics under high-density excitation, which is crucial for ultrafast optoelectronic devices.

### Value assessment

- **Mechanism clarification**
- By quantitatively combining experiment and theory, it clarifies the microscopic origin of the excitonic Mott transition under non-equilibrium conditions and corrects the limitations of traditional quasi-equilibrium models.

### One-sentence summary

This study proposes that the trARPES signals in WSe2 originate from a photo-induced transition between direct and indirect excitonic-insulating phases rather than excitonic quasiparticle scattering.

### Problem

Explaining the ultrafast (~30 fs) intervalley scattering observed in WSe2 trARPES and questioning the validity of the conventional excitonic quasiparticle picture in the non-linear regime.

### Approach

A combined theoretical and experimental approach using a non-equilibrium Williams-Lax method and self-consistent Hamiltonian to simulate the dynamical evolution of excitonic order parameters and lattice displacements driven by excited carriers.

### Main takeaway

The excitonic features in trARPES are single-particle levels renormalized by spontaneous excitonic polarization, with intervalley dynamics reflecting a transition from a direct to an indirect excitonic insulator.

### Why it matters

It provides a non-perturbative phase-transition perspective on ultrafast dynamics in TMDs and predicts accompanying coherent phonon oscillations, prompting a re-evaluation of the nature of excitons.

### Value assessment

### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

This study develops a unified theoretical description of cholesteric fingers in confined chiral media, analyzing their topology, energetics, and interactions.

### Problem

To clarify the internal structure, stability, and interaction mechanisms of cholesteric fingers (CF-1 and CF-2) as particle-like solitons in chiral liquid crystals and magnets.

### Approach

Using a Dzyaloshinskii-based continuum model including bulk anisotropy and surface anchoring, with numerical minimization to analyze isolated fingers and their periodic arrays.

### Main takeaway

Cholesteric fingers are composite solitons: CF-2 is a bimeron with unit charge, while CF-1 is a topologically trivial composite; their interaction is repulsive in a homogeneous background but becomes attractive in a conical background.

### Why it matters

It establishes a deep correspondence between solitons in liquid crystals and magnets, demonstrating that mixed finger sequences can provide a combinatorial platform for information encoding.

### Value assessment

- **Mechanism clarification**
- It clearly explains the meronic composition of fingers and the physical origin of interaction switching based on the background state.
