### One-sentence summary

The study reveals that on-site Coulomb repulsion and multi-orbital competition are key factors suppressing the transition temperature of exciton condensation in strongly correlated 2D semimetals.

### Problem

Explaining why the experimental condensation temperatures are low in strongly correlated materials like TMCs, despite their high predicted exciton binding energies.

### Approach

Using Cellular Dynamical Mean-Field Theory and Determinant Quantum Monte Carlo to simulate two- and three-orbital Hubbard models on a triangular lattice with strong on-site repulsion and inter-orbital attraction.

### Main takeaway

On-site Coulomb interaction significantly reduces the condensation temperature and alters its density dependence; meanwhile, competition between multiple pairing channels further suppresses condensation and induces orbital-selective excitonic states.

### Why it matters

This work provides a non-perturbative framework for understanding excitonic insulators in strongly correlated systems, helping resolve discrepancies between theory and experiment and guiding the design of high-temperature excitonic devices.

## Extended saddle points govern long-lived antiskyrmions

- **summarized_at:** `2026-03-20T09:12:04`

### Related topics

### One-sentence summary

The study reveals that anisotropic DMI enables extended saddle points that suppress entropic decay, leading to exceptionally long-lived nanoscale antiskyrmions in oxidized Fe3GeTe2.

### Problem

Achieving long-lived nanoscale magnetic solitons at room temperature is a major challenge, as their lifetimes typically decrease rapidly with temperature due to entropic effects.

### Approach

Developed a first-principles method for anisotropic DMI and used atomistic spin simulations combined with Harmonic Transition State Theory to study antiskyrmion stability in oxidized Fe3GeTe2 monolayers.

### Main takeaway

Extended saddle points induced by anisotropic DMI preserve translational zero modes and suppress entropic contributions to the pre-exponential factor, enhancing antiskyrmion lifetimes by over five orders of magnitude at room temperature.

### Why it matters

It establishes a new paradigm for enhancing soliton stability via transition-state geometry rather than just energy-barrier height, offering a new route for room-temperature stable spintronic devices.
