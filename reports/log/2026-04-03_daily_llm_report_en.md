### One-sentence summary

Investigates how an external magnetic field induces the quantum anomalous Hall effect in zero-magnetization Lieb-lattice altermagnets by breaking valley symmetry.

### Problem

How to realize and control the quantum anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets while maintaining vanishing net magnetization.

### Approach

Utilizing a tight-binding model on a Lieb lattice with d-wave altermagnetic order, Rashba SOC, and external magnetic fields to analyze topological phase diagrams via Berry curvature and Chern number calculations.

### Main takeaway

The external magnetic field breaks the rotational symmetry between X and Y valleys, enabling independent valley contributions that produce a quantum anomalous Hall state with a total Chern number of ±1 in a zero-net-magnetization system.

### Why it matters

The study provides a mechanism to rapidly switch topological states in altermagnets using only magnetic fields without strain or structural distortion, which is significant for developing magnetization-free topological electronic devices.

### One-sentence summary

The study finds that the antiferromagnetic Ising model on 1/1 Tsai-type approximants forms an ordered state with residual entropy via an 'entropic crystallization' mechanism.

### Problem

Exploring the low-temperature magnetic ordering patterns and underlying mechanisms in geometrically frustrated magnets, specifically Tsai-type quasicrystal approximants.

### Approach

Constructing an antiferromagnetic Ising model on a bcc lattice of icosahedra and using Monte Carlo simulations with parallel tempering to study its thermodynamic properties.

### Main takeaway

### Why it matters

It provides a theoretical basis for understanding complex magnetism in quasicrystal magnets and reveals a universal ordering mechanism driven by entropy rather than energy in strongly frustrated systems.
