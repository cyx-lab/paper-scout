## Engineering strong coupling with molecular coatings in optical nanocavities

### Related topics

exciton:exciton

### One-sentence summary

Molecular J-aggregate coatings on silver nanoparticles restructure the electromagnetic vacuum to enable strong coupling and Rabi oscillations for quantum emitters.

### Problem

Radiative dipole modes of bare metal nanoparticles lack sufficient coupling strength, while non-radiative modes cause quenching, hindering the observation of strong coupling and Rabi oscillations.

### Approach

Using macroscopic quantum electrodynamics with a Lorentzian pseudo-mode approximation to model the non-Markovian dynamics of emitters near core-shell plexcitonic structures.

### Main takeaway

A 2nm J-aggregate shell induces a 'geometric mode' that enables Rabi oscillations and multi-mode strong coupling at frequencies where bare nanoparticles only show exponential decay.

### Why it matters

It demonstrates a strategy to engineer quantum dynamics via molecular coatings without modifying the physical emitter-nanocavity distance, offering new design tools for nanophotonic devices.

## Symmetry-Driven Electrical Switching of Anisotropic Skyrmion Hall Effect in Altermagnets

- **pdf_url:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.17308v1
- **summarized_at:** `2026-03-19T09:26:10`

### Related topics

altermagnetism:altermagnetic

### One-sentence summary

Proposes a general strategy for the purely electrical switching of the anisotropic skyrmion Hall effect in 2D altermagnets by breaking crystal symmetry.

### Problem

Conventional skyrmion Hall effect control relies on power-hungry magnetic field reversal and is suppressed in antiferromagnets due to topological charge cancellation, limiting its spintronic applications.

### Approach

Revealed the mechanism through symmetry and model analysis, and validated the electric-field modulation of anisotropic interactions in a monolayer using first-principles calculations and atomistic spin simulations.

### Main takeaway

An external electric field induces sublattice-dependent anisotropic exchange and DMI, enabling an anisotropic skyrmion Hall effect whose transverse direction can be fully reversed by the field polarity.

### Why it matters

This study provides a theoretical foundation for field-free, high-precision control of skyrmion transport, which is crucial for developing low-power topological spintronic logic and memory devices.
