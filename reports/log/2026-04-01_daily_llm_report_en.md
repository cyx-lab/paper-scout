### Problem

### One-sentence summary

### Problem

### Approach

### One-sentence summary

This study reveals that residual hydrogen impurities in silicon nitride films fundamentally alter NIR dispersion by simultaneously modifying UV electronic transitions and MIR absorption via Kramers-Kronig relations.

### Problem

Conventional dispersion models assume material purity and neglect non-local dispersion shifts induced by residual impurities (like N-H bonds), leading to discrepancies between experiment and theory in broadband photonic device design.

### Approach

By performing sequential annealing on Si3N4 microresonators and using ellipsometry, FTIR, and multi-band vector spectral analysis, the authors quantified the impact of H-impurities on UV bandgap and MIR absorption, establishing a predictive physical model based on KK relations.

### Main takeaway

The NIR dispersion shift stems from the 'spectral tension' between UV bandgap blue-shift (positive GVD contribution) and MIR vibrational absorption (negative GVD contribution), reaching a balance point near 2 μm.

### Why it matters

This study provides more precise dispersion control for integrated photonic devices, which is crucial for developing octave-spanning soliton microcombs, supercontinuum generation, and quantum light sources.
